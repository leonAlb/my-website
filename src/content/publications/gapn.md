---
title: "The Non-phosphorylating Glyceraldehyde-3-Phosphate Dehydrogenase GapN Is a Potential New Drug Target in Streptococcus pyogenes"
date: "02/15/2022"
venue: "Frontiers in Microbiology"
url: "https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/microbiology/articles/10.3389/fmicb.2022.802427/full"
authors: "Eisenberg, Philip et al."
---

Streptococcus pyogenes lacks standard metabolic pathways, making the enzyme GapN its essential source of NADPH. Research confirms that targeting GapN effectively kills the pathogen, including drug-resistant strains, without harming bacteria that possess alternative pathways. This makes GapN an ideal target for precise, narrow-spectrum antibiotics.
